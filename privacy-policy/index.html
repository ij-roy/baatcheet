Privacy

Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how BaatCheet, developed by IJ Roy, handles user data for secure, lightweight chat and direct messaging.

Information BaatCheet may collect

  • Account information such as username, account email or identifier, and profile details provided in the app.
  • Username/password authentication information and related authentication/session records.
  • Messages, chat content, chat participation data, and conversation records needed to provide messaging features.
  • Media or files if you upload or send them through the app.
  • FCM push notification tokens used to deliver notifications.
  • Android ID or device identifiers stored for notification delivery, security, abuse prevention, or app operations.
  • Public key data used by app features that require account-level key records.
  • App diagnostics and basic operational data such as crash context, performance signals, logs, and backend request metadata.

How information is used

BaatCheet uses this information to create and secure accounts, authenticate users, deliver messages, store conversation participation records, send push notifications, maintain safety, prevent abuse, troubleshoot issues, and comply with legal or platform requirements.

Transmission and processing

User data is transmitted over HTTPS. Backend services and Firebase-related processing may be used for authentication, messaging operations, push notifications, diagnostics, storage, or other app infrastructure.

Sharing and retention

BaatCheet does not sell personal data. Data may be processed by backend, hosting, Firebase, analytics, diagnostics, or platform service providers where needed to operate the app. Data may be retained while an account is active and for a limited period where required for legal, safety, security, abuse-prevention, dispute, or platform compliance.

Account deletion

You can request deletion through the public BaatCheet account deletion page. Some messages/content already delivered to other users or stored in conversation records may be retained where needed for conversation integrity, safety, or legal compliance.
